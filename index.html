walking a darkness

i really love walking a darkness.

Walking in darkness has been an integral part of human existence since ancient times. In the earliest stages of human history, our ancestors ventured out into the night to hunt, gather, and explore. Their lives were intimately tied to the rhythms of day and night, and darkness offered both challenges and opportunities. In ancient civilizations, darkness held a mix of fear and reverence. It was often associated with the unknown, with dangers lurking in the shadows. Yet, it was also a time for reflection, storytelling, and spiritual practices under the canopy of stars. Throughout history, walking in darkness has played a significant role in literature and mythology. It symbolizes journeys of transformation and self-discovery, where heroes and seekers confront their fears and find hidden truths. From Odysseus navigating the dark seas to Dante's descent into the inferno, darkness has been a metaphor for the depths of the human psyche. In modern times, our relationship with darkness has evolved. Electric lighting has transformed our ability to navigate and work at night, yet it has also disconnected us from the natural rhythms of darkness. Walking in darkness now often means intentional acts of solitude, contemplation, and sometimes rebellion against the constant glow of screens and city lights.
